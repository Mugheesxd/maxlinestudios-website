Maxline Studios ("we", "us", "our") is a creative and digital production agency providing video editing, motion design, color grading, and related creative services to clients worldwide. When you submit a form, book a call, or contact us, we may collect: your name, email address, project details, budget range, preferred delivery timeframe, and any files or links you choose to share with us. We do not collect payment card details directly — payments are processed through third-party platforms with their own security standards.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide to respond to inquiries, prepare quotes, deliver contracted services, communicate about active projects, and improve our offerings. We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

Our website uses trusted third-party services to operate, including form processing (FormSubmit), scheduling (Calendly), and file hosting/preview (Google Drive) for delivering project previews. Project-related files and communications are retained only as long as necessary to complete your project and provide reasonable post-delivery support, typically no longer than 30 days after final delivery unless a longer archive period is agreed upon.

6. Cookies & Analytics

This website may use minimal, privacy-respecting analytics to understand aggregate traffic patterns (such as page views). No personally identifiable information is collected through analytics, and no data is used for cross-site advertising or tracking.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data. To exercise these rights, contact us at maxlinestudios@gmail.com and we will respond within a reasonable timeframe.

8. International Data

As we serve clients globally, information may be processed and stored using cloud infrastructure located in different countries. We take reasonable steps to ensure any such processing maintains an appropriate standard of protection consistent with recognized international data protection principles (including principles reflected in frameworks such as the GDPR and CCPA, where applicable).
